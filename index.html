About Me

PhD in Computational Chemistry from the University of Oxford with 3+ years of industry experience developing CADD tools and driving small-molecule and PROTAC projects from hit identification through lead optimization. Expert in executing physics-based methods (docking, MD, FEP) alongside building data-driven methodologies for structure- and ligand-based drug discovery.

Currently a Computational Chemist at Roche (China Innovation Center), leveraging generative models for de novo design and building ML workflows for virtual screening. Previously an EPSRC-funded doctoral researcher at Oxford, awarded the EPSRC Doctoral Prize for excellent impact of doctoral work.

Small-Molecule & PROTAC Drug Design

Generative AI for De Novo Design

Developed generative models for molecule generation and optimization. Applied pocket-based generative models to identify <10 μM hits for 3 targets. Built tools for macrocycle generation, fragment growing, and ring strain analysis.

Ultra-Large Virtual Screening

Deep learning-based virtual screening workflow for libraries of >100M compounds, reducing chemical search space by >80% while docking <0.5% of the library. Co-developed Uni-Dock (GPU-accelerated docking) and Uni-GBSA.

Virtual ScreeningDeep LearningUni-Dock

Membrane Protein & Transporter Simulations

MD and enhanced sampling to map thermodynamic energy profiles and passive diffusion mechanisms of small-molecule transporters. Investigated conformational dynamics of covalently linked molecules in membrane proteins.

MD SimulationsEnhanced SamplingFEP

Single-Molecule Sensing & Protein Engineering

Developed ML models to predict single-channel current levels in engineered biological membrane proteins. MD simulations of protein mutants to guide rational engineering of protein surfaces for single-molecule sensing.

Uni-GBSA & CADD Tool Development

Main developer of Uni-GBSA: an open-source automated package for high-throughput MM/GB(PB)SA calculations, integrated into a commercial drug discovery platform. Published as co-first author in Briefings in Bioinformatics.
